II · Puritan Settlement
Crossing to the New World
Gen IV

Thomas Betts I

1618 – 1688
Immigrant Patriarch
Mary · m. c. 1644

Sailed from England to Connecticut c. 1639 carrying a Bible printed in 1591. Co-founded Guilford, then became one of Norwalk's first settlers in 1660. Nine children. Given the honored "round seat" in the meeting house — the highest mark of respect.

Arrived Connecticut 1639 Founder of Norwalk Freeman 1664 Estate £146
Gen V

Thomas Betts II

1644 – 1717
Civic Leader & Assemblyman
Sarah Marvin · m. 1680

Selectman of Norwalk and elected Deputy to the Connecticut General Assembly across multiple sessions (1692–1707). Grew the family estate to £661. Addressed as "Mr." — a title reserved for men of high esteem.

CT General Assembly Selectman 1701 Estate £661
Gen VI

Thomas Betts III

1682 – 1761
Community Builder
Deborah

First Betts to live his entire life in Norwalk. Granted rights to build and operate the town grist mill in 1709 — vital infrastructure for the growing community. Expanded into Canaan Parish (later New Canaan).

Built Norwalk Grist Mill Canaan Parish Pioneer 79 years
III · Revolution
From Colony to Nation
Gen VII

Thomas Betts IV

1717 – 1787
Colonial Farmer & Father of a Patriot
Elizabeth "Betty" Benedict · m. 1748

Married into the prominent Benedict family. Raised six children through the French & Indian War and the gathering storm of revolution. Survived the devastating British burning of Norwalk in July 1779. Died in a free United States.

Survived Burning of Norwalk 1779 French & Indian War era
Gen VIII

Capt. Hezekiah Betts

1760 – 1837
Revolutionary War Hero
Grace Hanford · m. 1785

Joined the Continental Army and rose to Captain. Led a daring night assault at the Siege of Yorktown on October 21, 1781, capturing a British position and sustaining a serious wound — days before Cornwallis surrendered. Returned a war hero. Eleven children, two became ministers.

Siege of Yorktown 1781 Wounded in Action Continental Army Captain 11 Children
IV · Industry & Innovation
The Age of Invention
Gen IX

Henry Betts

b. 1794
Inventor & Industrial Chemist

Born in Norwalk to Hezekiah and Grace. Credited as the originator of the rotary printing press. Known as a noted inventor and chemist who also developed a process for making paper from straw.

Rotary Printing Press Paper from Straw Process Norwalk, CT
Gen X

Edgar Ketchum Betts

b. 1842
Industrialist · Troy, New York
Harriet Louisa Gardner

Moved to Troy, NY at age 14 and built a career in the collar manufacturing capital of America. Married Harriet, whose father Jefferson Gardner pioneered the collar industry and first applied sewing machines to collar production.

Troy "Collar City" Industry Earl & Wilson Co.
Gen XI

Anson Gardner Betts

1876 – 1976
Metallurgist & Scientist
Hattie Alice Ramsey

Yale Class of 1897, Columbia MA 1898. Invented the Betts electrolytic process for refining lead (patented 1901) — a landmark in industrial metallurgy. Developed zinc extraction processes. Authored Lead Refining by Electrolysis (1908). Lived to 100.

Betts Process — Lead Refining Yale & Columbia Zinc Electrolysis 1905 Published Author 100 years

Gen XII

Horace Gardner Betts

1921 – 2005
WWII Navy Veteran · USS MacKenzie
Gloria May Morton · m. 1950

Born in Hot Springs, NC. Enlisted in the U.S. Navy and served aboard destroyer USS MacKenzie (DD-614). Participated in convoy escorts, anti-submarine warfare, Operation Husky (Sicily), and Operation Dragoon (Southern France).

Operation Husky — Sicily 1943 Operation Dragoon — France 1944 USS MacKenzie DD-614 Battle of the Atlantic
